Montly food Chart.

Month: 7

♦️Fruits and Vegetables
• Examples: Spinach, sweet potatoes, bananas, berries, oranges. • Nutritional Level: High in vitamins (especially A and C), minerals (potassium, folate), and fiber. • Effects on Baby: Supports healthy growth and development, strengthens the immune system, and promotes proper organ development.

♦️Whole Grains
• Examples: Quinoa, brown rice, oats, whole wheat bread. • Nutritional Level: Rich in complex carbohydrates, fiber, B vitamins, and iron. • Effects on Baby: Provides sustained energy, helps with digestion, and contributes to brain development.

♦️Protein Sources
• Examples: Lean meats (chicken, turkey), fish (salmon, sardines), eggs, legumes (beans, lentils), nuts. • Nutritional Level: Essential amino acids, iron, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids. • Effects on Baby: Vital for tissue growth, muscle development, and brain function. Omega-3 fatty acids, in particular, are important for neurological development.

♦️Dairy or Dairy Alternatives
• Examples: Milk, yogurt, cheese, fortified plant-based milk (almond, soy). • Nutritional Level: High in calcium, vitamin D, protein, and probiotics. • Effects on Baby: Supports bone development and helps maintain maternal bone health.

♦️Healthy Fats
• Examples: Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds. • Nutritional Level: Monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats, vitamins (E, K), and fiber. • Effects on Baby: Supports brain development and helps with the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins.

♦️Nutritional Considerations
• Hydration: Staying well-hydrated is essential; aim for at least 8-10 glasses of water daily. • Iron and Calcium: Pay particular attention to iron (to prevent anemia) and calcium (for bone health). • Folic Acid: Continue to include folate-rich foods to support fetal neural development.
