Montly food Chart.

Month: 4

♦️ Fruits and Vegetables
• Examples: Spinach, oranges, bananas, carrots, berries. • Nutritional Level: Rich in vitamins (A, C, folate), minerals (potassium), and fiber. • Effects on Baby: Supports healthy development of organs, tissues, and the immune system; fiber aids digestion.

♦️Whole Grains
• Examples: Brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole wheat bread. • Nutritional Level: High in complex carbohydrates, fiber, B vitamins, and iron. • Effects on Baby: Provides sustained energy and essential nutrients for growth and brain development.

♦️Protein Sources
• Examples: Lean meats (chicken, turkey), fish (low-mercury options like salmon), eggs, legumes (beans, lentils). • Nutritional Level: High in protein, iron, zinc, and essential fatty acids. • Effects on Baby: Essential for tissue and muscle development, as well as brain growth.

♦️Dairy or Dairy Alternatives
• Examples: Milk, yogurt, cheese, fortified plant-based milk (soy, almond). • Nutritional Level: Rich in calcium, vitamin D, protein, and probiotics. • Effects on Baby: Supports bone health and overall growth.

♦️Healthy Fats
• Examples: Avocado, nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (chia, flaxseed), olive oil. • Nutritional Level: Contains essential fatty acids, vitamins (E, K), and energy. • Effects on Baby: Important for brain development and proper growth.

♦️Nutritional Considerations
• Hydration: Adequate fluid intake is important for overall health and can help with nausea. • Iron and Folate: Focus on iron-rich foods to prevent anemia and ensure adequate folate intake for fetal neural development.
