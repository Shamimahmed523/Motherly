Montly food Chart.

Month: 6

♦️Fruits and Vegetables
• Examples: Kale, sweet potatoes, bananas, oranges, berries. • Nutritional Level: High in vitamins (A, C, and folate), minerals (potassium, magnesium), and fiber. • Effects on Baby: Supports immune function, aids in organ development, and helps regulate digestion.

♦️Whole Grains
• Examples: Brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole grain bread, barley. • Nutritional Level: Rich in complex carbohydrates, fiber, B vitamins, and iron. • Effects on Baby: Provides sustained energy, essential nutrients for fetal growth, and supports brain development.

♦️Protein Sources
• Examples: Lean meats (chicken, turkey), fish (low-mercury options like salmon), eggs, legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas). • Nutritional Level: High in protein, iron, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids. • Effects on Baby: Crucial for muscle and tissue development, brain growth, and overall fetal health.

♦️Dairy or Dairy Alternatives
• Examples: Yogurt, milk, cheese, fortified plant-based alternatives (soy, almond). • Nutritional Level: High in calcium, vitamin D, protein, and probiotics. • Effects on Baby: Supports bone and teeth development and helps regulate the mother’s calcium levels.

♦️Healthy Fats
• Examples: Avocado, nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (chia, flaxseed), olive oil. • Nutritional Level: Contains essential fatty acids, vitamins (E, K), and energy. • Effects on Baby: Important for brain development and overall growth, and supports nutrient absorption.

♦️Nutritional Considerations
• Hydration: Adequate fluid intake is vital for managing swelling and supporting overall health. • Iron and Calcium: Emphasize iron-rich foods to prevent anemia and ensure adequate calcium for bone development. • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Include sources like fatty fish and flaxseeds for optimal brain development.
