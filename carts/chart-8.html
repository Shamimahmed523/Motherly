Montly food Chart.

Month: 2

♦️ Fruits and Vegetables
• Examples: Kale, carrots, berries, apples, oranges. • Nutritional Level: High in vitamins (A, C, K), minerals (potassium, magnesium), and fiber. • Effects on Baby: Supports immune function, promotes healthy skin and organ development, and aids in digestion for both mother and baby.

♦️Whole Grains
• Examples: Barley, brown rice, whole grain pasta, oats. • Nutritional Level: Rich in complex carbohydrates, fiber, B vitamins, and iron. • Effects on Baby: Provides energy and supports fetal growth and brain development.

♦️Protein Sources
• Examples: Lean meats (beef, chicken), fish (preferably low-mercury options like salmon), eggs, tofu, legumes (beans, lentils). • Nutritional Level: High in protein, iron, zinc, and essential fatty acids. • Effects on Baby: Crucial for muscle and tissue development, brain growth, and overall fetal health.

♦️Dairy or Dairy Alternatives
• Examples: Yogurt, milk, cheese, fortified almond or soy milk. • Nutritional Level: High in calcium, vitamin D, protein, and probiotics. • Effects on Baby: Supports bone development and helps regulate the mother's calcium levels.

♦️Healthy Fats
• Examples: Avocado, nuts (walnuts, almonds), olive oil, seeds (chia, flaxseed). • Nutritional Level: Provides essential fatty acids, vitamins (E, K), and energy. • Effects on Baby: Important for brain development and overall growth.

♦️Nutritional Considerations
• Hydration: Continue to prioritize hydration, as it aids in digestion and helps manage swelling. • Iron and Calcium: Focus on iron-rich foods to prevent anemia and ensure adequate calcium for bone health. • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Include sources like fish and flaxseeds to support brain development.
